White Papers

A detailed or authoritative report. This is a commonly used term to refer to a wide array of documentation. Specific to this Knowledgebase, it will refer to all of our technical documents that are available to provide information in different delivery methods. There are several information delivery methods outlined below, each with unique strengths and weaknesses.

Quick Reference Guides

Quick Reference Guides are a new work in progress. The main use case for these whitepapers is as a product insert - a single sheet of paper that a customer can read after unboxing a product to understand the contents of the product, and simple instructions to make connections.

Quick Start Guides

Quick Start Guides are longer than reference guides, and are intended to walk a customer through all of the necessary steps for a successful, initial deployment of a product or system.

Data Sheets

Just the facts. Data sheets (sometimes referred to as 'cut sheets') have an emphasis on the raw specifications of a device. It should contain A&E (Architectural and Engineering) information, as well as a brief product description. Useful to provide to customers inquiring about product capabilities.

Manuals

The whole enchilada. It should contain all technical specifications, warnings, and information about installation and usage of a product. While useful for reference, more times than not, the manual is "too much" information.

How-To Notes

How-To notes cover the installation or operation of a single topic. For a device with 1000 functions, a How-To note will emphasize and embellish on the successful operation and usage of a single function.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions. Information is presented in a Q&A type format, where the most common questions are in a simple list, with the possible reasons and outcomes listed as 'Answers', or even just suggested troubleshooting steps when explicit answers cannot be identified immediately.
